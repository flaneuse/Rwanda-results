The USAID GeoCenter, located in the US Global Development Lab, conducted the Rwanda Livelihoods Analysis and Mapping over course of six months in 2016 – 2017.
The study incorporated numerous data sets including:
  • The 2010 and 2014/15 Demographic and Health Survey
  • The 2012 and 2015 Comprehensive Food Security and Vulnerability Analysis from the World Food Program
  • The Famine Early Warning System Network (FEWS NET) livelihood zones shapefile
  • The WorldPop raster data on human population distributions
  • The 2002 and 2012 Rwanda Population and Housing Census
  • Spatial data from the National Institute of Statistics of Rwanda, Natural Earth, and Esri
The team used R-Studio, Stata, ArcGIS, QGIS, Excel, D3.js, and Adobe Illustrator to process, analyze and visualize the data. The code for all analysis is available under the MIT License. If you find it useful, please let us know.
