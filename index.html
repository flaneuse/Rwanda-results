Rwanda: a growing nation
Rwanda’s extraordinary recovery from complete political, economic and social collapse after the 1994 genocide represents one of Africa’s most dramatic and encouraging success stories. Annual economic growth rates are among Africa’s highest, and huge strides have been made in social indicators such as child and infant mortality, household income, and primary-school enrollment levels.
Nevertheless, Rwanda remains among the world’s poorest and most densely populated countries.

Where in the world is Rwanda?
Rwanda is situated in the middle of this major population cluster surrounding Lake Victoria. Even without being situated directly on the lake, Rwanda is a major population center.
Rwanda is the densest nation in Africa
Though individual cities are more concentrated, almost all of Rwanda — even outside the urban areas — has high population density. Indeed, Rwanda has the highest population per square kilometer3 (471 people / km2) in all of Africa.
What are the implications of high population density?
High population growth has profound impacts on Rwanda's ability to continue to develop.
